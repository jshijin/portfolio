GoodLife Fitness - Optimize Gym Utilization and Class Attendance through Predictive Modeling

This project aims to enhance the utilization of fitness classes and gym equipment at GoodLife Fitness through predictive modeling. Predicting the class attendance and crowd levels will enable dynamic scheduling and better facility management,theerby improving overall member satisfaction.

Highlights

  • Achieved 76.8% accuracy with SVM, enabling dynamic adjustments in class availability based on predicted attendance.
  • Used Random Forest with an R² of 0.90 to predict gym crowd levels, improving facility management.
  • Applied cyclical encoding for time-based features, improving temporal pattern recognition in gym crowd predictions.
  • Fine-tuned SVM parameters through grid search, achieving a cross-validation accuracy of 78%.

AI-Driven Predictive Modeling for Traffic Accident Severity on USA Highways

This project harnesses machine learning to predict traffic accident severity across the US, providing critical insights for improving road safety and reducing fatalities.

Highlights

  • Leveraged a comprehensive dataset of over 7.7 million traffic accidents across 49 states in the USA to predict accident severity.
  • Achieved 92% accuracy in predicting accident severity using the Random Forest classifier.
  • Addressed class imbalance by retraining the model with higher weights assigned to underrepresented severity classes, improving its predictive performance for severe accidents.
  • In-depth EDA was done which revealed critical patterns, such as the concentration of accidents in specific cities and the correlation between weather conditions and accident severity.
